Welcome to Clearfolio

Clearfolio is a self-hosted household net worth tracker. It lets you record periodic snapshots of your assets and liabilities, visualise growth over time, and project your financial future.

First-time setup

  1. Add your assets — bank accounts, investments, superannuation, property, and anything else of value. Go to Assets and click Add Asset.
  2. Add your liabilities — mortgages, loans, credit cards, student loans. Go to Liabilities and click Add Liability.
  3. Record your first snapshot — go to Snapshots and record current values for each asset and liability. You can also use Bulk Entry to backfill historical data.
  4. Set a goal — go to Settings → Goals and set a net worth target to track progress against.
  5. Set up cashflow — go to Cashflow and add your income streams and expenses to track your contribution rate.

Ongoing use

Come back each quarter (or however often you like) and record new snapshot values. Your dashboard will automatically update with trends, breakdowns, and goal progress.

Important information

Clearfolio is a personal tracking tool designed to help you monitor your household finances. It does not provide personal or general financial advice.

Projections and calculations are illustrative only and based on assumptions that may not reflect actual outcomes. Past performance is not a reliable indicator of future performance.

You should seek independent advice from a licensed financial adviser before making financial decisions.

Key Concepts

Assets & Liabilities

Assets are things you own that have value — bank accounts, shares, super, property. Liabilities are debts you owe — mortgages, personal loans, credit cards, student loans. Your net worth is assets minus liabilities.

Asset Categories

Each asset type belongs to a category that determines how it appears in charts:

  • Cash — savings accounts, offset accounts, term deposits
  • Investable — shares, ETFs, managed funds
  • Retirement — superannuation
  • Property — real estate. Your primary residence (PPOR) may qualify for the main residence CGT exemption, though this can be partial if the property was income-producing or has land exceeding 2 hectares
  • Other — vehicles, collectibles, etc.

Snapshots & Periods

A snapshot records the value of an asset or liability at a point in time. Snapshots are grouped by periods — quarterly intervals based on either the Australian Financial Year (FY) or Calendar Year (CY).

Australian Financial Year

The Australian financial year runs from 1 July to 30 June. Quarters are:

  • FY Q1 — July to September
  • FY Q2 — October to December
  • FY Q3 — January to March
  • FY Q4 — April to June

For example, FY2026-Q3 means January–March 2026.

Ownership

Assets and liabilities can be sole (owned by one member) or joint (shared with a configurable split). When viewing by member, joint items are apportioned according to the split ratio.

Views

Use the view toggle in the navbar to switch between Household (everything combined) and individual member views. This filters all pages — dashboard, cashflow, projections — to show only that member's share.

Scopes

The dashboard lets you toggle between three net worth scopes:

  • Total — all assets and liabilities
  • Financial — excludes property (useful for tracking investable wealth)
  • Liquid — only immediately accessible assets

Debt Classification

Liabilities are classified by their tax treatment to help you understand your debt profile:

  • Income-producing — debt tied to income-producing assets where interest may be tax-deductible (e.g. investment property loan, margin loan)
  • Non-deductible — debt where interest is generally not tax-deductible (e.g. home loan on your primary residence, HECS-HELP, tax liability)
  • Consumption — debt used to fund spending rather than asset acquisition (e.g. personal loan, car loan, credit card)

ASX Price Data

Assets with an ASX symbol display live price quotes sourced from Yahoo Finance. Price data may be delayed and is not suitable for trading decisions. When you add a symbol, Clearfolio also calculates a historical annualised return (compound annual growth rate) and volatility from up to 5 years of weekly price data to pre-fill your projection settings.

Features

Dashboard

Your financial overview. Shows net worth stat cards, goal progress, cashflow summary, and charts organised into tabs — Trends (net worth over time), Composition (asset/liability breakdowns), and Comparison (member and super balance comparison).

Assets

Manage your assets. Each asset has a type, owner, and optional ASX symbol for live price quotes. You can set contribution amounts and expected return rates used by the projection engine.

Liabilities

Track your debts. Each liability has a type, owner, optional interest rate, and repayment schedule.

Cashflow

Track income streams and expenses. Shows annualised totals, contribution rate, debt-to-income ratio, and category breakdowns. Contributions are pulled automatically from your asset configuration.

Snapshots

Record asset and liability values over time. Use single entry for quick updates or Bulk Entry to backfill multiple periods at once. This is the data that powers your dashboard trends.

Projections

Model your financial future with three methods:

  • Compound — deterministic projection using each asset's expected return rate and contributions
  • Scenario — pessimistic, base, and optimistic bands
  • Monte Carlo — probabilistic simulation using return rates and volatility

Settings

Configure household details, manage members, set net worth and super targets, manage expense categories, and import/export data.

PDF Export

Export a comprehensive PDF report from the dashboard. Includes all stat cards, charts, member comparisons, and goal progress.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is the goal projection calculated?

The dashboard goal projection uses the compound growth engine — the same model as the Projections page. It runs a 30-year forward projection using each asset's expected return rate, contributions, and liability repayments, then finds the year where your net worth crosses the target.

What's the difference between Financial and Liquid net worth?

Financial net worth excludes property — it shows your investable and accessible wealth. Liquid net worth goes further, only counting assets you can access immediately (cash and short-term investments).

How do ASX symbols work?

When you add an ASX symbol to an asset, Clearfolio fetches price quotes from Yahoo Finance (data may be delayed) and calculates a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) and volatility from up to 5 years of historical weekly prices. These values pre-fill your expected return rate and volatility for projections. Price data is not suitable for trading decisions.

How often should I record snapshots?

Quarterly is the default cadence (matching the period system), but you can record as often as you like. More data points give better trend analysis and more accurate goal projections.

What does the contribution rate measure?

Contribution rate = Total Annual Contributions ÷ Total Annual Income. It shows what percentage of your take-home pay you are actively directing into assets (e.g. super, ETFs, savings accounts). Income should be entered as take-home pay — after tax and salary sacrifice. Many financial commentators suggest a rate above 20% as a benchmark, though the right level depends on your circumstances.

Can multiple people use this?

Yes. Clearfolio supports multiple household members via Cloudflare Access authentication. The primary member can add others in Settings → Members. Each member's email must be allowed in your Cloudflare Access policy.

Where is my data stored?

All data is stored locally in a SQLite database on your server (typically a Raspberry Pi). Nothing is sent to external services. You own your data completely.

How do I back up my data?

Go to Settings → Data → Export to download a JSON file containing all your household data. Store this somewhere safe. You can restore from a backup using the Import function.
